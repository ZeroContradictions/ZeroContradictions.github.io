1. Why I’m Writing This Blog Post

In my opinion, the term “virtue-signaling” should’ve been first coined by Ted Kaczynski, and it’s a shame that he wasn’t the one to invent the term. He had a golden opportunity to invent the term and become known for inventing it when he was addressing it in his 1995 manifesto “Industrial Society and Its Future”. He repeatedly wrote in the manifesto that he just couldn’t think of a better term to describe what he was trying to talk about. Kaczynski hit the nail on the hammer that [virtue signaling] is largely a result of the Power Process being disrupted in industrial-technological society, but unfortunately he referred to “virtue signaling” as “leftism” since the term had not been invented yet and he hadn’t thought of it. This naturally drew a lot of criticism from leftists, but it would have drawn less hostile criticism if he would have been the first to coin the neologism, especially since it would’ve communicated a more specific meaning and would’ve been more easily differentiable as a different concept.

There is a lot I could say about semantic fields and how the invention of new words for specific concepts leads to faster neurological retrieval of those concepts, but writing about it would overwhelm me at the moment. I have experience with lexicography and writing dictionaries, so I feel that that makes me especially qualified to write this glossary.

By coining my own neologisms, it will help to popularize this blog since it will gain more notoriety for being the first or among the first sources to have ever used the term, if it catches on in popular language.

2. Epistemological Neologisms That I Shall Coin Or Popularize

  • Reasoning Skills: the same thing as critical thinking skills, but better wording in every way
  • Empirically-Grounded Coherentism: Coherentism where beliefs are ultimately based one’s own empiricism.
  • Epistemological Nexus: (Currently no definition exists, but this term could be used to identify or describe something that previously had no specific name.
  • Solidified Intelligence: The same thing as crystalized intelligence
  • Metaphysical Free Will (MFW): Determinism is false (this is the opposite of determinism). This does not exist and can be disproven by proof by contradiction.
  • Subconscious Epistemic Free Will (SEFW): The ability for you to subconsciously choose something, regardless of whether you can actually do it or not, and also regardless of whether determinism is true or false. Having subconscious choices is the exactly same thing as having needs, desires, or preferences. If SEFW exists, then value also exists. ALL sentient organisms have SEFW.
  • Conscious Epistemic Free Will (CEFW): The ability for you to consciously choose to do what you want, regardless of whether you can actually do it or not, and also regardless of whether determinism is true or false. CEFW is more complex than SEFW since it requires more than just merely desiring things that would satisfy one’s needs/desires. It also requires understandingly what exactly that you want, and knowing how to get it.
  • Attainable Free Will (AFW): The two conditions that: 1. you can choose to do as you want, and 2. you are able to do it within your current circumstances. Since being conscious is a requirement in order to be ’capable’ of doing something, Attainable Free Will has to be conscious by definition, and it would be the case that Subconscious Attainable Free Will does not exist. Likewise, Conscious Attainable Free Will is the exact same thing as AFW.
  • “We don’t know what we don’t know” / Plato’s Problem Clause: “According to all available evidence and my current knowledge, this is the correct conclusion | the correct conclusion is: …”
  • *Humble Skepticism (or some other better term?): The willingness to be open to new ideas and the ability to admit when one is wrong.
  • The Three Great Illusions: 1. Supernatural Gods, 2. Free Will, 3. Objective Morality (this loaded-term encompasses a number of things: the common absence of intuition to question the legitimacy of life/government/morals/cultural practices/etc). There will be some difficulty to enumerate more “illusions” since many illusions could be considered as more specific variants of the already stated illusions.

  • Selfishness: i
  • Egoism: i
  • Moral Accounting System: i
  • Cynicalism: Selfishness, except that you take what you can, and give nothing back.

By explicitly defining “Selfishness” and “Cynicalism”, I can contrast the two definitions to show what “Selfishness” is not what most people think it is.

3. Human Nature / Axiology Neologisms That I Shall Coin Or Popularize

  • Post-Ideological: i
  • Post-Moral: i
  • Over-Socialized: i
  • Cooperationist Economics: Economic policies that promote the greatest production possible according to the post-moral cooperationist ethics (and as such, a system of land rent and citizen’s dividend is implied)
  • Productionist Eugenics: Eugenics based upon someone’s ability to contribute to society.
  • Pro-Diversity Eugenics: Eugenics that aim to increase instances of favorable genetic diversity rather than reducing genetic diversity.
  • Reason System: A proposed alternative to democracy that might work better in some cases, or the name of debate software that I was conceptualizing about that would facilitate better debating
  • Law of Egoism: Corollary to the Law of Biological Evolution.
  • Unintelligent Design: i
  • Rationalized: attempting to make something seem reasonable, even if actually isn’t. This term can be applied to both metaphysics/epistemology or ethical / political philosophy, and while it can always describe someone’s attitude or actions towards facts about the former, it seems less applicable to apply it to people’s ethical or political opinions since morality is subjective, and most of the time when people do rationalize their ethical or political opinions, it is typically done in an attempt to justify oneself according to the Law of Egoism, in which case self-justification or self-rationalization may be better terms.
  • Self-Justification / Self-Rationalization: The psychological tendency for life to justify its own existence, beliefs, and actions for the sake of its own continued existence, even if their beliefs are wrong, their existence doesn’t accomplish anything meaningful according to rational beings, and their actions work against post-moral cooperation ethics.
