1. Introduction: “Bullshit Jobs” by David Graeber

[Introduction to the book and its arguments]

The Wikipedia Article on “Bullshit Jobs”

The book ought to be at least somewhat right, even more so when considering the vast quantity of how much academic research is fake, however the author’s proposal for UBI definitely wouldn’t be a good idea at all, as decisively argued by BG in his essay “The Case Against UBI”.

2. The Book’s Arguments Are Western-Centric

Although the author credits the Puritan and Capitalist views and work ethic for the existence of Bullshit Jobs, this view of his seems to be very Western-centric. East Asian and Confucian values for example also seem to be very responsible for long hours worked in East Asia.

For example, overworking in South Korea was so unnecessary and so overdone that South Korea had to pass a law capping the maximum number of hours that workers work each week.

The problem in those cultures is that the value of hard working, even when it leads to no practical wealth, is still highly valued. Since the culture, social status, and probably also the genetics work in favor of this inefficient use of labor, the government is the only institution left that can do anything about the problem. It would be nice if it wasn’t necessary to use the state to fix this problem, but the reality is that the state has become necessary to regulate this when all else has failed and all else has no realistic chance of fixing the problem in a deterministic world.

3. The Book Doesn’t Address People Who Gain Wealth Via Rent-Seeking

Some people are harshly overworked when their labor actually produces real wealth in the world, while others do “work” that accomplishes essentially nothing, not to mention people like land speculators who don’t have to work at all.

5. Are Most Bullshit Jobs Created By The Private Sector, Or The Public Sector?

Additionally, I would challenge the argument that “most bullshit jobs are created by the private sector, not the government”. I probably wouldn’t disagree with that statement if we were only going by raw numbers because it makes sense that the private sector would produce bullshit jobs over all since the private sector is several times larger than the public sector.

However, there should be no doubt that the public sector produces LOTS of bullshit jobs too. Some examples: people who just stand around doing nothing in the army or military because they don’t have anything to do, teachers who teach fake knowledge (I have lots of examples that I could write about in another essay), bloated education boards, unneeded bureaucracy, university professors who spend many hours lecturing when such lectures could be recorded on video instead, the obvious corruption and corporatocracy that plagues the government, etc.
