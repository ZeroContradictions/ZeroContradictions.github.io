2. Evolutionary Mismatch

There are essentially two main ways to solve evolutionary mismatch.

We can imagine two distinct worlds where are humans living in an environment that they were evolutionarily designed to live in, and the second world that is completely changed by technology where humans have been genetically engineered and re-program to better fit the world of technology since technology society and culture change faster than evolution ever can. and the other world to me my imagined as an in between state where humans are living in a world that has progressed very far in terms of technology but the humans haven’t evolved enough nor have been engineered enough to fit in the world which is roughly where humanity has been since at least the beginning of the industrial revolution.

4. Technology Causes Just As Many Problems As It Solves

The only thing that technology is good for is replacing old problems with new problems. And frankly, the new ones are just as bad as the old ones (if not now, then eventually), and arguably worse in some cases.

Today’s problems are yesterday’s solutions.

  • This could be interpreted from an anarcho-primitivist viewpoint, or a libertarian perspective on government.

Technology is just as much of a problem as the malevolent people who use it.

6. The Effects Of Technology On Human Evolution

It is interesting to think of all the effects that vaccines have had on evolution. When decided if a new technology or social change should be made to the population, we should be asking if it will be worth the impact that it will have on human evolution.

  • For example, vaccines are worth implementing since they don’t significantly increase the dysgenics of the population, for as long as it is possible to mass produce vaccines for all humans, and all livestock.
  • On the other hand, a public healthcare system is a bad idea since it subsidizes unhealthy people and unhealthy lifestyles, whereas allowing natural selection would make it possible to get rid of dysgenic genes that make people more likely to smoke, more likely to become obese / sedentary, more likely to get cancer, etc.

If many or most people who begin to use a new technology to make their lives easier or more productive eventually become dependent on that technology(ies), maybe the goal of technological advancement isn’t necessarily to find an easier way of doing things, but rather to become dependent on a better way of doing things rather than dependent on a less productive or less efficient way of doing things.

Although every industrializing society has gone through difficult, labor-intensive times for the working class, those times would not have been as stressful or difficult as they were had Georgism been installed in those societies.

Reconcile incompatibilities, transitioning between humans, environment, evolution, and technology with the current modern abundance of resources and expansion of carrying capacity in order to avoid living in zero-sum circumstances of life.
