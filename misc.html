Philosophy of Language / Linguistics

  • I have a Linguistics BA, hence why I have a whole section dedicated to posts talking about language and linguistics.

  • The Consequences of a Constructed Universal Auxiliary Language (Worldlang)

    I explain the effects that a Universal Auxiliary Language would have on the world, why it's a better solution to language barriers than building universal translator devices, the practicality of undertaking the establishment of such a phenomenon, and what would be needed to make it happen.

  • Mathematical Methods for Modeling Phonetics & Phonology

    This is the senior thesis I wrote for my Mathematics BS. Language phonology can be modeled using set theory, string theory, combinatoritcs, finite-state transducers, and calculus-based statistics.

  • Maximum Entropy Phonotactics by Bruce Hayes and Colin Wilson

    Maximum Enropy Phonotactics uses more advanced mathematics than Optimality Theory, but it also models language phonology with even greater accuracy.

  • Philosophy of Definitions and Semantics

    TBA: I haven't started writing this post yet.

  • Advantages And Disadvantages To Logographic Writing Systems

    Logographic writings draw their advantages from linking meanings to writing, rather than sounds to writing, as a phonetic writing system would. From a linguist's perspective, they both have their strengths, and neither is better than the other.

  • The Best Way to Learn Logographic Writing Systems: Heisig's Method

    Heisig's Method is the best way to learn Hanzi and Kanji since it separating the learning process into two phases: memorizing the characters and themselves and their semantics, and learning how to pronounce the characters and their various connotations. I explain why this is the superior and most efficient approach.

  • Heuristic Strategies for Identifying Etymology in English and Various Other Languages

    Being able to recognize patterns in pronunciation, spelling, and grammar are characteristic of a language's sources of vocabulary can help one identify/predict the etymological origins of a language's lexicon with high accuracy.

  • Book Summary: Through the Language Glass

    StateOfTheNihil gives a summary of the book, Through the Language Glass, which is probably worth reading, unless you have a linguistics degree.

  • Corpus Linguistics, The Mathematics Behind It, And Its Impact On Knowledge Processing

    Corpus linguistics is useful for analyzing how frequently we think about various ideas and memes to one another. Collocations have a major effect on our cognitive processing.
