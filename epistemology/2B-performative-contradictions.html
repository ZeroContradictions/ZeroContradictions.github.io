1. The Logical Axioms And Performative Contradictions

1.1. List Of Logical Axioms (Postulates)

  • Law of Identity
  • Law of the Excluded Middle
  • Law of Non-Contradiction

1.2. List Self-Contradictory Statements Used To Prove The L2 Theorems

  • Existence: I do not exist.
  • Consciousness: I am not conscious.
  • Aliveness: I am dead / I am not alive.
  • Determinism: See the proof on this proof by performative contradiction at this link here.
  • Epistemic Free Will: I cannot choose anything. / I will not to will.
  • True Statements Exist: All statements are false.
  • Invalidity of Skepticism/Solipsism: One cannot be certain of anything; one can only be certain that one exists.
  • Validity of Reason: Reason is not a valid source of knowledge.
  • It Is Good To Have Values: People should not have values / Having values in of itself is a bad thing / A world with no value would be preferable (more valuable) over a world with value generated by sentient beings.

2. List Of L2 Theorems (Proven By The Contradictions That Are Inherent In The Previous Statements)

  • Existence Theorem 2.1: I do not exist.
  • Consciousness Theorem 2.2: I am not conscious.
  • Aliveness Theorem 2.3: I am dead / I am not alive.
  • Epistemic Free Will Theorem 2.4: I cannot choose anything. Note: ’Epistemic Free Will’ is defined differently from ’Metaphysical Free Will’, which does not exist.
  • True Statements Exist Theorem 2.5: All statements are false.
  • Invalidity of Total Skepticism/Solipsism Theorem 2.6: One cannot be certain of anything; one can only be certain that one exists.
  • Validity of Reason Theorem 2.7: Reason is not a valid source of knowledge.
  • Good Values Theorem 2.8: It is good to not have values / People should not have values / Having values in of itself is a bad thing / A world with no value would be preferable (more valuable) over a world with value generated by sentient beings.

3. The Justification For Sense Perception

The justification for sense perception is similar to the justification for reason. Reason is the only thing that we have, so if we reject reason, then we have nothing. Likewise, if we reject empiricism and insist that we cannot trust our senses to the extent that they are not impaired or illusioned, then we don’t have any way of receiving any other sort of input from reality.

So we don’t exactly have a foundation for proving empiricism and sense perception (to the extent that it is valid). We have a brain, and we have senses. And we must choose to use both of them because that’s all we have. Fortunately, evolutionary reasoning can also justify empiricism, just as it can justify reason, which helps build coherency. It should be self-evident to anybody who rejects the existence of supernatural phenomena that sense perception is the only source of input that humans have for observing and learning about reality.

4. The Justification For Reason

Just because it is a performative contradiction to use reason to reject the validity of reason, that still doesn’t necessarily mean that everybody must follow reason because evidently they can still reject reason, even if it’s irrational. This also does not imply that reason is a firm foundation for belief either, especially since reason is dependent on one’s sense perception and experiences beforehand. It is just as much of a fallacy for someone to believe that they can pull reason out of logic, which is very much what the performative contradiction does, hence other methods are needed to validate and clearly explain why reason is the only valid source of sensory knowledge.
