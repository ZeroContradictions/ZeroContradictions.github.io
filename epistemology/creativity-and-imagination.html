1. Creativity

Creativity is defined as the production of original ideas, imagination, and/or artistic work. In this context, creativity is distinguished from intelligence/genius.

2. The Origin Of Creativity: “What Spawns Original Creativity?”

  • Creativity can spawn either by answering questions or by making connections.
  • People create inventions to solve problems. And inventions can only be created when there is the necessary desire and creativity present to solve said problems.
    • When the wheel was first invented, it was because someone asked, “How can I transport this big heavy stuff from point A to point B?”
      • That someone had a problem that needed to be solved, so they created an answer to it, the wheel.
  • Creativity may also spawn when people make connections.
    • This method is certainly artistic, but it is often not entirely original on its own since this method requires borrowing previous creativity and applying it somewhere else.
  • Creativity will spawn wherever there is a need or a desire for it to exist.

3. Imagination

  • Imagination is defined as the faculty or action of forming new ideas, or images or concepts of external objects not present to the senses.
  • Imagination is the origin of all concepts that do not exist and/or are not possible in reality.
  • If everything that exists in reality can ultimately be derived from perceiving reality with our senses, then everything that doesn’t exist in reality but can be imagined/conceptualized is ultimately derived from our imagination since it couldn’t have originated from anywhere else, especially not from reality. Faulty reasoning is the only other way that concepts that don’t exist in reality can arise.
  • Imagination is the formulation of concepts that may or may not be possible in reality.
