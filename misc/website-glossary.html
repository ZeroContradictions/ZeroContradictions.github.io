1. Why I’m Writing This Glossary

In my opinion, the term “virtue-signaling” should’ve been first coined by Ted Kaczynski, and it’s a shame that he wasn’t the one to invent the term. He had a golden opportunity to invent the term and become known for inventing it when he was addressing it in his 1995 manifesto “Industrial Society and Its Future”. He repeatedly wrote in the manifesto that he just couldn’t think of a better term to describe what he was trying to talk about. Kaczynski hit the nail on the hammer that [virtue signaling] is largely a result of the Power Process being disrupted in industrial-technological society, but unfortunately he referred to “virtue signaling” as “leftism” in his manifesto since the term had not been invented yet and he hadn’t thought of it. This naturally drew a lot of criticism from leftists, but it would have drawn less hostile criticism if he would have been the first to coin the neologism, especially since it would’ve communicated a more specific meaning and would’ve been more easily differentiable as a different concept.

On the other hand, Richard Dawkins coined the term “meme” so that he could better describe a new field of knowledge that is related to evolution and biology, and he gained a lot of fame since he was the first person to invent the neologism and use it to communicate a very important and interesting phenomenon for describing how the world works.

There is a lot I could say about semantic fields and how the invention of new words for specific concepts leads to faster neurological retrieval of those concepts, but writing about it would overwhelm me at the moment. By coining my own neologisms, it will help to popularize this blog since it will gain more notoriety for being the first or among the first sources to have ever used the term, if it catches on in popular language.

Many of the terms defined on this webpage were not invented by myself, but they are displayed here anyway for quick reference, and so that newcomers to this website can learn its jargon quickly.

Metaphysical Free Will (MFW)
Determinism is false (MFW is the opposite of determinism). This is essentially the same thing as Libertarian Free Will, which does not exist in reality, and can be disproven by proof by contradiction.
Subconscious Epistemic Free Will (SEFW)
The ability for you to subconsciously choose something, regardless of whether you can actually do it or not, and also regardless of whether determinism is true or false. Having subconscious choices is the exactly same thing as having needs, desires, or preferences. If SEFW exists, then value also exists. ALL sentient organisms have SEFW.
Conscious Epistemic Free Will (CEFW)
The ability for you to consciously choose to do what you want, regardless of whether you can actually do it or not, and also regardless of whether determinism is true or false. CEFW is more complex than SEFW since it requires more than just merely desiring things that would satisfy one’s needs/desires. It also requires understandingly what exactly that you want, and knowing how to get it.
Attainable Free Will (AFW)
The two conditions that: 1. you can choose to do as you want, and 2. you are able to do it within your current circumstances. Since being conscious is a requirement in order to be ’capable’ of doing something, Attainable Free Will has to be conscious by definition, and it would be the case that Subconscious Attainable Free Will does not exist. Likewise, Conscious Attainable Free Will is the exact same thing as AFW.

Post-Moralism
The philosophical position that game theory is the best way to understand morality. It is similar to moral relativism since it posits that morality can only be perspective-dependent, but it’s different since it also embraces the game-theoretic assumptions of selfishness and rationality.

Selfishness

Altruism
Acting for the benefit of others.
Cynicalism
Selfishness, except that you take what you can, and give nothing back. A cynical person does not use a moral accounting system.
  • NOTE: I am aware that I am defining Cynicalism in a non-standard way, but I am doing so because I feel that the most common definition of “Cynicalism” is too closely related to the definition for “Selfishness”, and I feel that “Cynicalism” would be better used to convey a shade of meaning that most people tend to mistakenly associate with the headword “Selfishness”. By explicitly defining “Selfishness” and “Cynicalism”, I can contrast the two definitions to show what “Selfishness” is not what most people think it is.
Moral Accounting System (MAS)
The system of emotions in a human’s brain (and some other species’ brains too) that motivates people to do give-and-take behavior, or exchanges of labor. Evolution caused the MAS to develop in humans since they are more reproductively successful when they engage in give and take behavior within human tribes. The MAS is the primary reason why people are not cynical, and the behavior that MASs encourage does not count as “reciprocal altruism” since “reciprocal altruism” is an oxymoron.
Collaboration
When rational individuals work together for mutual benefit. When people are collaborating, the group is never places above the individuals for the sake of the group. Cooperation requires trust (the expectation of reciprocity), and every individual who collaborates does so with the expectation that they will get something in return.
Collectivism
When individuals place the collective above the individual for the sake of the collective.
Utopian Ideology
Any naive ideology that people will believe in order to claim moral superiority and maintain the illusion of knowledge. Utopian Ideologies typically use the Rhetoric of Exploitation, simple-minded worldviews, moral narratives, visionary fantasies, and effective memetic propagations to delude people and spread the ideology.
