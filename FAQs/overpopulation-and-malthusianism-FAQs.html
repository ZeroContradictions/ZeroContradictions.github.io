1. How is overpopulation a threat to humanity? Hasn’t Malthusianism been debunked?

No, Malthusianism has not been debunked. The simplest way to disprove Cornucopianism (the opposite of Malthusianism) is to point out that it is absurd to insist that there can be infinite population growth within a finite environment.

Moreover, just because we haven’t face overpopulation yet, that doesn’t mean that it will never happen. Everything that was thought impossible, but was possible, only seemed impossible until it actually happened. Even though Thomas Malthus’s predictions on the world’s maximum population have been proven wrong so far ever since the Industrial Revolution began, that doesn’t make Cornucopians right because it’s equivalent to saying “Well I haven’t died yet, so I will never die”, even though that is absurd. Everybody knows that life cannot live forever, and likewise, everyone ought to know that infinite growth is impossible within a finite environment.

For more information, see: Malthusianism Vs Cornucopianism: Why Cornucopianism is Wrong

6. But humanity is nowhere close to overpopulation!

That is false. There are plenty of signs and examples of overpopulation in various regions of the world:

  • In 2022, the world is currently facing a global food shortage caused by extreme heats, droughts, the Russian invasion of Ukraine (which caused supply shortages for wheat, corn, vegetable oil, and more), and other factors. 7. How would population control affect family structures and people’s lives?

Since population control would cause us to move from the traditionally high fertility levels that human societies have historically had to relatively low fertility levels, this would necessitate redefining the role of women in society, compared to what it has been traditionally (raising children and housekeeping). Since a society with population control would also be a society where women raise fewer children on average, this would mean that women would be spending more time doing other things, like working paid jobs in the economy.
