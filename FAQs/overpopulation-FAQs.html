2. How is overpopulation a threat to humanity? Hasn’t Malthusianism been debunked?

No, Malthusianism has not been debunked. The simplest way to disprove Cornucopianism (the opposite of Malthusianism) is to point out that it is absurd to insist that there can be infinite population growth within a finite environment.

Moreover, just because we haven’t face overpopulation yet, that doesn’t mean that it will never happen. Everything that was thought impossible, but was possible, only seemed impossible until it actually happened. Even though Thomas Malthus’s predictions on the world’s maximum population have been proven wrong so far ever since the Industrial Revolution began, that doesn’t make Cornucopians right because it’s equivalent to saying “Well I haven’t died yet, so I will never die”, even though that is absurd. Everybody knows that life cannot live forever, and likewise, everyone ought to know that infinite growth is impossible within a finite environment.

2.1. Preventing Overpopulation

IF:

  1. The population is projected to surpass the carrying capacity at some point in the near future, AND
  2. the carrying capacity cannot be foreseably increased,

THEN: There should be population limit controls, ideally before the population reaches the carrying capacity as some buffer room to prevent things like water shortages and such. There doesn’t seem to be any forseeable solutions as to how future generations will resolve these drastic clean freshwater shortages, among other problems.

3. How is Population Control Related to Eugenics?

When a population reaches the carrying capacity of its environment, competition and eugenic selection occur in order to select the organisms that are the most reproductively successful in the scarce resources environment.

By removing the selectionary pressures on humanity and limitations that have traditionally limited the carrying capacity of the human population, not only does this cause dysgenics from the removal of said selectionary pressures, but it also causes overpopulation. That said, if we achieved legally-enforced population control for humanity, then we would be placing artificial selectionary pressures that will increase the eugenic quality of the human population, that would otherwise never occur.

If someone is going to be in favor of population control, ought to be in favor of bottom-up eugenics as well (top-down eugenics would fail the same reason why command economies fail). Many people today are against eugenics because it would require restricting some people from having children, but population control does the same thing and population control will be necessary in order to prevent catastrophic disasters from happening in the future, so this is not a good argument against eugenics.

5.1. Overpopulation Can Happen Instantly And Unpredictably

What most people don’t realize about overpopulation is that’s it’s possible for it to happen both instantly and unpredictably if a major catastrophe happens, just like the Irish Potato Famine (1845-1849). Once the potato was introduced to Ireland, the country had a population explosion since the crop increased the number of available calories and nutrition to the population.

Real Life Lore Video: Why Ireland Has Fewer People Than 200 Years Ago

Nobody would’ve predicted in 1843 or 1844 that the island would be infected with a blight within the next year that would cause a massive famine where a million people die and another million have to emigrate. One year, the island was not overpopulated because it had enough resources for everybody. The next year, it was facing famine and mass exodus. That’s how quickly overpopulation could potentially happen, if the catastrophe causing it is severe enough.

The key to causing overpopulation to have a new staple crop or technology that causes a population boom, followed by some event which disables the stimuli / resources that were responsible for the population boom. Although the population growth leading up to an overpopulation event might happen gradually, the catastrophe that leads to the overpopulation can happen instantly and unpredictably. All it takes is a major and/or unprecendented catastrophe, and everything changes. Since it’s always possible for an unpredictable colossal catastrophe to cause instant overpopulation, that means that ideally we should always be prepared against a potential overpopulation crisis.

The dumb people will outbreed the smarter people, unless smarter people start having more children.

Second, Atheists are ignoring the concept of Adaptive Coherence. Basically, genes and memes work together, so if memes like religion are currently causing religious people to have more children, then the genes that are associated with higher religious beliefs are going to get reproduced with greater frequency than genes that make people more likely to be Atheist and such.

Third, Atheists are further ignoring that memes can be reasonably categorized into “Traditions” and “Fashions/Cults”. Religions would be categorized as “Traditions” because they propagate from parent to child and tend to increase reproductive success, whereas memes like Feminism and Antinatalism would be categorized as fashions because they tend to propagate between peers, not from parents to children. Fashions like Feminism and Antinatalism may be temporarily increasing in popularity now, but they neither promotes reproductive success, so they are both destined to decline in their numbers of believers, given enough generations passing by. Fashions tend to be temporary phenomena, whereas Traditions are stable phenomena because they tend to outlast Fashions in the long-term.

20. What would the world population be if the black plague never happened?

I once had a history teacher who estimated that that the current population of Europe would be 10 times greater if the Black Plague never happened, but this uses fallacious reasoning. There’s no way that could ever happen anyway since that would require increasing the carrying capacity of Europe by ten-fold as a result of the black plague never happening.

Yes, the Black Plague did reduce the European population by about a third, but since biological populations are selected to reproduce up to the carrying capacity, it’s more likely that the black plague only temporarily widened the gap between the current population and its maximum carrying capacity. Since there were temporarily more resources per capita available to all the living humans due to the massive decline in population, this means that the surviving humans were likely able to have more children than previous generations. Thus, the European population would’ve returned to the carrying capacity irregardless of whether the plague ever happened.

21. What will Humanity’s Next Overpopulation Crisis look like?

The next major overpopulation crisis will probably happen when a catastrophic event causes the carrying capacity to suddenly and unexpectedly shrink, rather than the population surpassing a stagnant carrying capacity. If this happens, it’ll probably less likely to be caused by a blight or disease in the food supply since modern agricultural techniques probably use more genetically diverse crops, but we still cannot underestimate the potential for such massive catastrophes. Instead, the next major event that could cause a worldwide overpopulation crisis is more likely to be a massive war (which restricts the food supply for much of the world via embargoes, blockades, destruction of food production).

The people of today who are not concerned with overpopulation and ridicule Malthus for being wrong (so far) tend to believe that overpopulation would look like the Earth’s population and the Earth’s resources both increasing (the latter due to technological advancements), with Earth’s population increasing faster. Not only does this ignore the possibility that a major/unprecendented catastrophe could cause overpopulation to happen instantly, but it’s also less likely than the catastrophe-caused scenario since a severe catastrophe could happen at any moment.

All it will take to prove that Neo-Malthusianism concerns are/were warranted is for an unprecendented emergency to happen. For example, suppose the following series of plausible geopolitical events occurs:

  1. China invades Taiwan,
  2. The US military defends Taiwan,
  3. India takes the opportunity to attack China and seize Tibet while China is distracted,
  4. Japan and South Korea support Taiwan’s defense as well in order to protect their international trading routes through the Strait of Taiwan,
  5. North Korea might takes the opportunity to attack South Korea while they are distracted,
  6. Every country around the world picks a side (China vs the US),
  7. WWIII starts,
  8. massive food shortages begin everywhere due to the disruption of free trade, and
  9. the world faces unprecedented overpopulation due to all of the aforementioned.

This is a very realistic scenario, especially amid the recent rapid growth of the Chinese military and increasing Chinese aggression around Taiwan. The Russian invasion of Ukraine shouldn’t leave the world doubting that China could follow suit and invade Taiwan whenever it chooses. If this or a similarly catastrophic scenario occurs, Neo-Malthusianism will be proven right eventually. Once again, History shows that overpopulation can happen unpredictably within mere months, so anyone would be incredibly naive to think that it’s never something to worry about.
