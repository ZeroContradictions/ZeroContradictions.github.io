1. How is overpopulation a threat to humanity? Hasn’t Malthusianism been debunked?

No, Malthusianism has not been debunked. The simplest way to disprove Cornucopianism (the opposite of Malthusianism) is to point out that it is absurd to insist that there can be infinite population growth within a finite environment.

Moreover, just because we haven’t face overpopulation yet, that doesn’t mean that it will never happen. Everything that was thought impossible, but was possible, only seemed impossible until it actually happened. Even though Thomas Malthus’s predictions on the world’s maximum population have been proven wrong so far ever since the Industrial Revolution began, that doesn’t make Cornucopians right because it’s equivalent to saying “Well I haven’t died yet, so I will never die”, even though that is absurd. Everybody knows that life cannot live forever, and likewise, everyone ought to know that infinite growth is impossible within a finite environment.

For more information, see: Malthusianism Vs Cornucopianism: Why Cornucopianism is Wrong

2. How is Population Control Related to Eugenics?

When a population reaches the carrying capacity of its environment, competition and eugenic selection occur in order to select the organisms that are the most reproductively successful in the scarce resources environment.

By removing the selectionary pressures on humanity and limitations that have traditionally limited the carrying capacity of the human population, not only does this cause dysgenics from the removal of said selectionary pressures, but it also causes overpopulation. That said, if we achieved legally-enforced population control for humanity, then we would be placing artificial selectionary pressures that will increase the eugenic quality of the human population, that would otherwise never occur.

If someone is going to be in favor of population control, ought to be in favor of bottom-up eugenics as well (top-down eugenics would fail the same reason why command economies fail). Many people today are against eugenics because it would require restricting some people from having children, but population control does the same thing and population control will be necessary in order to prevent catastrophic disasters from happening in the future, so this is not a good argument against eugenics.

For more information, see: EPC and Reproduction Licenses FAQs

3. How is Georgism Related to Population Control?

Georgist reasoning compliments the population control scheme that we want to implement extremely well, and not by coincidence if we expand the notion of “natural resources” to also include the finite supply of organisms that can exist within a finite environment. Since life is a zero-sum competition game for energy when a population reaches its carrying capacity, anybody who has children in such an environment would be doing so at the cost of preventing other people to have children as well. This is very similar to how anybody who occupies a parcel of land prevents other people from using that parcel. 4. Why is Demographic Transition Theory incorrect?

Here’s the long answer.

TL;DR: The religious fundamentalists and other people with the highest fertility rates will out-compete the rest of the entire world, and the genes and memes that caused child-free people to choose not to have children will die out. The end result will be that the genes that caused higher fertility rates will become more populous, and this will cause the world population to skyrocket once more until it crashes against the carrying capacity ceiling. The world population will not max out at 11-12 billion people, and if it does, that will only be because the world population reached the carrying capacity of the Earth.

4.1. But doesn’t that reasoning conflate memes with genes?

No, and for several reasons:

First off, religious people tend to be dumber than Atheists. So if dumb hyper-religious people are having more children than smart Atheists, then the world’s average IQ is projected to decline (it’s already started), which will make the world population more susceptible to becoming more religious again. The dumb people will outbreed the smarter people, unless smarter people start having more children.

Second, Atheists are ignoring the concept of Adaptive Coherence. Basically, genes and memes work together, so if memes like religion are currently causing religious people to have more children, then the genes that are associated with higher religious beliefs are going to get reproduced with greater frequency than genes that make people more likely to be Atheist and such.

Third, Atheists are further ignoring that memes can be reasonably categorized into “Traditions” and “Fashions/Cults”. Religions would be categorized as “Traditions” because they propagate from parent to child and tend to increase reproductive success, whereas memes like Feminism and Antinatalism would be categorized as fashions because they tend to propagate between peers, not from parents to children. Fashions like Feminism and Antinatalism may be temporarily increasing in popularity now, but they neither promotes reproductive success, so they are both destined to decline in their numbers of believers, given enough generations passing by. Fashions tend to be temporary phenomena, whereas Traditions are stable phenomena because they tend to outlast Fashions in the long-term.

7. How would population control affect family structures and people’s lives?

Since population control would cause us to move from the traditionally high fertility levels that human societies have historically had to relatively low fertility levels, this would necessitate redefining the role of women in society, compared to what it has been traditionally (raising children and housekeeping). Since a society with population control would also be a society where women raise fewer children on average, this would mean that women would be spending more time doing other things, like working paid jobs in the economy.
