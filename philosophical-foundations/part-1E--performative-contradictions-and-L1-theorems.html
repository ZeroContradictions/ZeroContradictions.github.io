  • There is no objective morality. -There HAS TO be an error here in why this is NOT a valid performative contradiction.
    • The reason why this one does not work is because morality is intrinsically perspective dependent. Even if the person saying this statement might believe in objective morality, there can only be objective morality from their perspective at most, based on whatever values the believer has.
    • More generally, this shows that performative contradiction does not work if the thing in question is perspective-dependent.
    • Another reason why this statement does not prove objective morality is because claiming the non-existence of objective morality does not have to implicitly assume the existence of objective morality. Stating that “nothing is objectively immoral” is merely the statement of an objective fact.
    • It can be more clearly shown why there is no contradiction in this statement if we rephrase the statement with two other statements that have exact equivalent meaning:
      • There is no objective morality. = Nothing is objectively immoral. = All morality is relative.

3. Thinking Some More About Additional Performative Contradictions

In his book The Logical Leap: Induction in Physics (which he co-wrote with physicist David Harriman), Peikoff argues that there is no problem of induction, because philosophy is itself an inductive science and, therefore, that any attempt to deny the validity of induction contradicts itself by implicitly accepting the validity of induction. Peikoff and Harriman also argue that scientific claims verified by induction should be considered true until new evidence warrants modifying or amending them because scientific knowledge derived from induction is contextual.

In other words, those who on the basis of conclusive evidence make inductive scientific claims regarding science cannot argue that their claims are subject to no possible modification, but can argue that they are the only rational claims that can be believed based on the available evidence. They conclude that the same process of induction is essential to every rational field (except mathematics) and that, as a result, truth in any such field possesses the same objectivity as that of physics.

It is a performative contradiction to “know that you don’t know”. It is NOT a contradiction to “know what you don’t know”.

Blocking a phone number still means that the other end CAN receive your messages, just not your calls. And if you block them, you can’t receive read receipts, so you wouldn’t know whether they received their receipts or not. This can create the false illusion that you can’t send any messages to the blocked person. You can send the messages, you just can’t know if they received them or not.

4. Why It Is A Performative Contradiction To Believe That People Can Have Free Will Independent Of Their Materialistic Minds

  • Given that we live in a deterministic universe and we obviously already know what it’s like to live in one, what would a non-deterministic universe be like to live in?
  • By definition, this would mean that life would have free will that is not bound to how its DNA or brains are programmed.
  • Free will only concerns life. In our materialistic universe, life is matter that was created with the perfect mix of chemicals to be able to do all its functions and behave the way it does.
  • In other words, in an idealist universe, it would be possible to create something that behaves just as life does in our universe, but is able to also not behave as life does in our universe.
  • Such a concept would probably be deserving of a different word other than “life”. Perhaps it could be “frilife” (meaning free-will life).
  • The minds of frilife would be able to act independently of how they are materialistically structured. No matter what structure frilife has, it would be acting independently according to how some supernatural force desires.
  • The structure of the minds of frilife would therefore be completely useless and irrelevant (aside from sending signals throughout the body necessary to maintain basic life-sustaining processes like temperature regulation, pain, senses, et cetera).
  • But there’s a problem. If frilife was acting independently to how its mind is structured, what else would it be controlled by to decide how it should behave and function? Another materialistic structure? No, because the premises rule that out as impossible. And there is no other known force in the universe that could make it act independently of its structure. In conclusion (according to everything we currently know obviously), frilife is impossible because it creates a metaphysical contradiction.
  • By trying to imagine such as a concept, the text above collectively forms a Proof by Contradiction. Though it was unintentional to write a proof, this is a very interesting proof.
  • Likewise, this is why religious people are able to believe that free will exists. They make the mistake of imagining a supernatural power that would make it the case that the contradiction above never exists, thus free will would be able to exist. But the reality is that this is not the case because they assumed a false premise in the first place.

5. The Ultimate Justification For Sense Perception

The ultimate justification for sense perception is similar to the justification for reason. Reason is the only thing that we have, so if we reject reason, then we have nothing. Likewise, if we reject empiricism and insist that we cannot trust our senses to the extent that they are not impaired or illusioned, then we don’t have any way of receiving any other sort of input from reality.

So we don’t exactly have a foundation for proving empiricism and sense perception (to the extent that it is valid). We have a brain, and we have senses. And we must choose to use both of them because that’s all we have.

The necessary justification of sense perception will be important to note after establishing the L1 Theorems and defining the reasoning process, perhaps when in the subsection header titled Sense Perception.

It should be self-evident to anybody who rejects the existence of supernatural phenomena that sense perception is the only source of input that humans have for observing and learning about reality.

The acceptance of reason, sense perception, and that reproduction is the ultimate biological value can each be justified not only evolutionary theory, but also on the grounds that if they are not accepted, then there’s not really any other alternatives (although there are millions of alternatives to valuing reproduction, it’s still the most reasonable one from a biological standpoint and also because it aligns well with psychological intuitions).

NOTE: The basis for cooperation ethics for greater prosperity might ultimately be justified somewhat objectively per se by the same reasoning that is used to ultimately justify the validity of senses, validity of reasoning, and procreation as the ultimate purpose of life.

Fortunately, evolutionary reasoning can also justify empiricism, just as it can justify reason, which helps build coherency.
